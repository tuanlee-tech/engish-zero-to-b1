# Unit 6 - I don’t … (Present Simple Negative)

## A - Grammar

The Present Simple negative is **don’t / doesn’t + verb**.

**Examples:**

- She **doesn’t** drink coffee.
- He **doesn’t** like his job.

### Positive vs Negative

| Subject             | Positive                | Negative                                       |
| ------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| I / we / you / they | work, like, do, have    | **don’t** (do not) work / like / do / have     |
| he / she / it       | works, likes, does, has | **doesn’t** (does not) work / like / do / have |

**Examples:**

- I drink coffee, but I **don’t** drink tea.
- Sue drinks tea, but she **doesn’t** drink coffee.
- You **don’t** work very hard.
- We **don’t** watch TV very often.
- The weather is usually nice. It **doesn’t** rain very often.
- Sam and Chris **don’t** know many people.

### Remember:

- I/we/you/they **don’t** …
- he/she/it **doesn’t** …

**More examples:**

- I **don’t** like football.
- He **doesn’t** like football.
- I **don’t** like Fred and Fred **doesn’t** like me.
- My car **doesn’t** use much petrol.
- Sometimes he is late, but it **doesn’t** happen very often.

### Important note:

We use **don’t / doesn’t + infinitive** (don’t like / doesn’t speak / doesn’t do etc.):

- I **don’t** like washing the car.
- Sarah speaks Spanish, but she **doesn’t** speak Italian.
- David **doesn’t** do his job very well.
- Paula **doesn’t** usually have breakfast.

**cost - go - know - rain - see - use - wear**
